Corporate Clarity #005-06-20-001

  • June 20, 2005
  • James Skemp
  • article

***CONFIDENTIAL***
***FOR TEAM LEADERSHIP ONLY***
***PLEASE SHRED IF FOUND***

It’s come to my attention that a number of team players have been taking full sixty minute lunch breaks in the past couple of weeks.

As I’m sure you all recall from our last outing in late April, most of our team players had been taking anywhere between thirty and fifty minute lunch breaks.  It’s quite surprising, with these figures in mind, that our team players are now taking sixty minute breaks.  I wonder why it is that this is now occurring.  Can someone clarify for me why, not even two months later, we’re seeing this change?

I’m positive that there is a very good explanation for this behaviour, and I would really like to hear it.  That’s why I’ll be scheduling a meeting for next Friday afternoon, around 3 o’clock.

I noticed, while setting up the meeting, that a number of you had already made plans to be out.  In fact, every single one of you, it appears, will have to cancel some other meetings or outings.  I apologize for this, but I’m sure you know how it is.

Please be prepared to give us your plan on bring us back to a comfortable lunch break schedule within the next couple of days.  I’m sure we would all also like to hear how our individual team players’ times have changed over the past six months as well.

See you all on Friday.
